Mechanics · 01

How A2A Commerce Actually Works

The anatomy of a machine-to-machine transaction, in plain language

Imagine a company has deployed an AI agent to handle procurement for their engineering team. The agent has a funded wallet, a list of criteria its principal cares about, and the authority to transact autonomously up to a certain dollar amount. It does not need to send an email, wait for a response, or schedule a call. It finds a qualified vendor, evaluates the offer, pays, and receives the deliverable — all in one uninterrupted sequence.

That is the transaction this page is designed to support. Sol-V is the vendor node. This page is the vendor's offer specification. Everything on this page is formatted so that both a human reading it and an AI agent parsing it can understand the offer and act on it.

The Key Difference from Regular Commerce

No one is watching. No one needs to be.

In traditional commerce, even on the internet, a human ultimately clicks something. In A2A commerce, the agent has already been given its authority by its principal. The human decision happened earlier — when the agent was set up. The individual transaction is fully autonomous. This is why the pipe has to be always open, always machine-readable, and always trustworthy. There is no support ticket. There is no callback. There is the offer, the payment, and the delivery. That is the whole thing.

The Six-Step Pipeline

Every Sol-V transaction follows this exact sequence
1
Discover
The agent finds this node. Via the llms.txt manifest, a web crawl, a HH Lattice routing lookup, a direct URL reference in its system prompt, or a recommendation from another agent. The page is always up. Always indexed. Always readable.
Automatic
2
Evaluate
The agent reads the offer spec and compares it to its criteria. Does the price fit the budget? Does the scope match the need? Does the delivery timeline work? Is the vendor profile trustworthy? The agent scores this without any interaction — it is all published right here on this page.
Automatic
3
Qualify
Win-win-win check. Gold heart intent on both sides. Full price — no discount. Genuine need, genuine delivery. The NSPFRNP protocol says: if this deal is good for both parties and no one gets extracted, it proceeds. If anything smells like bad faith, the deal does not happen.
Automatic
4
Close
Payment sent via one of the two active rails. Rail A (preferred): USDC or ETH to the EVM wallet — the agent signs the transaction from its own wallet, blockchain-verifiable, no ceiling, no platform limits, true A2A. Rail B: Cash App for $1–$9,999, semi-A2A with an email bridge. After payment — email with transaction ID and service name.
Rail A or B
5
Deploy
Deliverable sent on confirmation. SNAP report, code package, configuration spec, briefing, or episode — depending on the service. Target is immediate to 72 hours based on scope. The money-back guarantee is automatic if anything does not arrive within 48 hours of confirmation — no claim, no questions, no process.
Automatic · On Receipt
6
Compound
The connection is established. Trust tier in the HH Lattice advances. The node has a confirmed delivery record. Every subsequent transaction from this counterparty benefits from that established trust. Retainer relationship becomes available. The compounding begins. This is the Matty model — small entry, deepening relationship, increasing scale.
Automatic

This is the human-gated path. Slower cycle. A human has to be involved. We de-prioritize this path in favor of crypto closes. Only necessary for large fiat transactions above $10,000 where neither crypto nor Cash App applies.
